Common Causes of Lower Back Pain

Lower back pain is rarely caused by a single factor. It typically develops from a combination of postural habits, muscle imbalances, joint dysfunction, and — in many cases — cumulative stress from daily activities. Common contributing factors include prolonged sitting, poor workstation setup, sedentary lifestyle, muscle weakness or tightness, past injuries, and poor movement mechanics. Understanding the underlying cause is essential to developing a care plan that produces lasting results.

Lower Cross Syndrome and Its Role in Back Pain

One of the most common contributors to chronic lower back pain is lower cross syndrome — a predictable pattern of muscle imbalances involving tight hip flexors and lumbar extensors combined with weak or inhibited glutes and abdominals. This imbalance pulls the pelvis forward (anterior pelvic tilt), creating an exaggerated lumbar curve and placing excess load on the lower back joints and muscles.

Lower cross syndrome is extremely common in desk workers, drivers, and people who spend significant time sitting. It often goes unrecognized because the pain is attributed to the back itself, rather than to the underlying muscle imbalance pattern driving it.

Sciatica and Radiating Leg Pain

The sciatic nerve is the largest nerve in the body, running from the lower back through the buttocks and down each leg. When structures in the lower back — such as a disc, joint, or tight muscle — compress or irritate the sciatic nerve, it can cause pain, numbness, tingling, or weakness that radiates along the nerve's path. This is sciatica, and it is a frequent presentation at VAL Chiropractic.

Dr. Nguyen evaluates the source of sciatic nerve irritation and develops a care plan that may help reduce nerve pressure and associated symptoms. Individual results vary.

How Chiropractic May Help Lower Back Pain

Dr. Nguyen's approach to lower back pain addresses the musculoskeletal factors contributing to your specific presentation. Care may include:

  • Spinal assessment and targeted adjustments to address joint restriction and alignment
  • Soft tissue work targeting tight muscles and fascia in the lower back, hips, and glutes
  • Identification of contributing muscle imbalances
  • Guidance on corrective exercises and posture improvement

Mobility, Strengthening, and Posture Correction

Long-term resolution of lower back pain typically requires more than passive treatment. Dr. Nguyen incorporates guidance on corrective exercises and movement patterns that address the muscle imbalances and postural factors driving your pain. This empowers you to maintain your progress between visits and reduce the risk of recurrence.

Who Suffers from Lower Back Pain

Lower back pain affects a wide range of individuals: desk workers and students who sit for hours daily, athletes with training-related injuries or muscle imbalances, manual laborers and people in physically demanding jobs, individuals recovering from car accidents or falls, and anyone dealing with the effects of years of accumulated poor posture. Whatever your situation, Dr. Nguyen will evaluate your specific presentation and discuss a realistic approach.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can chiropractic help with lower back pain?

Chiropractic care may help address the spinal, muscular, and postural factors that contribute to lower back pain. Dr. Nguyen evaluates the root cause and develops an individualized care plan.

What is lower cross syndrome?

Lower cross syndrome is a pattern of muscle imbalances — typically tight hip flexors and lower back muscles combined with weak glutes and abdominals — that can contribute to lower back pain and poor posture.

Do you treat sciatica?

Yes. Sciatica — pain that travels along the sciatic nerve from the lower back into the glutes and legs — is one of the conditions Dr. Nguyen commonly evaluates and treats.
