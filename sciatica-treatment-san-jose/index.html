What Is Sciatica?

Sciatica refers to pain caused by irritation or compression of the sciatic nerve — the longest and widest nerve in the human body. It originates in the lower back (lumbar spine), travels through the buttocks, and extends down each leg. When the sciatic nerve is irritated or compressed, it can cause pain, numbness, tingling, and weakness along its path.

It is important to note that sciatica is a symptom — a description of where pain is felt — not a diagnosis in itself. The underlying cause of sciatic nerve irritation must be identified to develop an effective care plan.

Symptoms of Sciatica

Sciatica can present differently depending on the location and nature of nerve irritation. Common symptoms include:

  • Pain in the lower back, buttock, or leg — often on one side
  • Sharp, shooting, burning, or electric pain radiating down the leg
  • Numbness or tingling in the leg, foot, or toes
  • Muscle weakness in the affected leg
  • Pain that worsens with prolonged sitting or certain movements
  • Pain that improves when lying down or walking

Common Causes of Sciatic Nerve Pain

Sciatic nerve irritation can result from several underlying conditions, including disc herniation or bulge in the lumbar spine pressing on the nerve root, piriformis syndrome (a muscle deep in the glutes that can compress the sciatic nerve), lumbar joint dysfunction or spinal stenosis, and lower cross syndrome contributing to structural stress on the lumbar spine and pelvis. Identifying the specific cause is the foundation of effective treatment.

Chiropractic Evaluation for Sciatica

Dr. Nguyen performs a thorough evaluation including a detailed history, physical and neurological examination, and assessment of lumbar spine function, hip mechanics, and nerve tension tests. This evaluation helps identify the source of sciatic nerve irritation and guides the care plan. Referral for imaging may be recommended when appropriate.

Treatment Approach at VAL Chiropractic

Dr. Nguyen's approach to sciatica care may include lumbar spinal evaluation and targeted adjustments designed to reduce joint restriction and nerve pressure, soft tissue work addressing the piriformis and surrounding hip musculature, assessment of contributing postural and muscle imbalance factors, and guidance on positions, activities, and exercises that may support recovery. Individual results vary.

When to Seek Care

If you are experiencing leg pain, numbness, or tingling that you suspect may be sciatica, it is worth getting evaluated promptly. While many cases of sciatica improve with conservative care, severe or progressive neurological symptoms — such as significant weakness or loss of bladder or bowel control — require immediate medical evaluation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does sciatica feel like?

Sciatica typically involves pain that travels from the lower back through the buttocks and down one or both legs. It may feel like a sharp, burning, or electric sensation. Numbness and tingling are also common.

Can a chiropractor help with sciatica?

Chiropractic evaluation may help identify contributing factors to sciatic nerve irritation, and care may be designed to help reduce nerve pressure and associated pain. Individual results vary.

How long does sciatica last?

Duration varies depending on the cause and severity. Some cases improve within weeks with appropriate care; others take longer. Dr. Nguyen will evaluate your specific situation.
