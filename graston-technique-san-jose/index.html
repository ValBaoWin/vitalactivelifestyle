What Is Graston Technique?

Graston Technique is a form of instrument-assisted soft tissue mobilization (IASTM) — a category of manual therapy that uses specially designed stainless steel tools to detect and treat areas of soft tissue dysfunction. The tools are designed to help clinicians locate areas of fascial restriction, scar tissue, and fibrosis, then apply controlled pressure to address these findings.

IASTM techniques like Graston are often used as part of a broader rehabilitation or chiropractic care plan — not as a standalone treatment. The goal is to help break down scar tissue and fascial adhesions, restore normal soft tissue extensibility, and support recovery from injury.

Common Uses for IASTM Techniques

  • Chronic tendinopathy (Achilles, patellar, rotator cuff)
  • Post-surgical or post-injury scar tissue
  • Fascial restrictions limiting range of motion
  • Plantar fasciitis and foot/ankle issues
  • IT band friction syndrome
  • Lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow)
  • Post-accident soft tissue injuries

How IASTM Fits Into a Care Plan

Instrument-assisted soft tissue work is most effective as part of a comprehensive care plan that addresses the underlying mechanical and movement factors contributing to the condition. At VAL Chiropractic, soft tissue techniques are integrated with chiropractic adjustments and corrective exercise guidance as appropriate for each patient's specific presentation. Individual results vary.

What to Expect from IASTM Treatment

IASTM treatment involves the clinician using the tools to scan the tissue and identify areas of restriction, then applying controlled pressure in targeted areas. Treatment may produce temporary redness or bruising in treated areas — this is expected and indicates tissue engagement. Discomfort during treatment is typically mild to moderate, and Dr. Nguyen will communicate with you throughout to ensure your comfort.
