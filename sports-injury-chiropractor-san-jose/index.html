Chiropractic Care for Athletes and Active Adults

Sports injuries are one of the most common reasons active people seek chiropractic care. Whether you're dealing with an acute injury from a specific incident or a nagging overuse problem that has been limiting your performance, Dr. Lam Nguyen at VAL Chiropractic is experienced in evaluating and treating a wide range of athletic injuries.

Dr. Nguyen takes a whole-body approach to sports injury care — looking at how the injured area relates to the rest of your kinetic chain, identifying movement imbalances that may have contributed to the injury, and developing a plan designed to support both recovery and long-term performance.

Common Sports Injuries We Treat

  • Shoulder injuries: Rotator cuff strains, shoulder impingement, AC joint injuries, and overhead athlete issues
  • Hip pain: Hip flexor strains, IT band syndrome, sacroiliac joint dysfunction, and piriformis syndrome
  • Knee pain: Patellofemoral syndrome, IT band friction syndrome, patellar tendinopathy, and post-sprain rehabilitation
  • Ankle sprains: Lateral ankle sprains, chronic ankle instability, and return-to-sport rehabilitation
  • Lower back pain: Lumbar strains, disc irritation, and sport-specific back injuries
  • Neck pain: Cervical strains from contact sports, forward head posture from cycling or triathlon positions

Soft Tissue Therapy and Mobility Work

Many sports injuries involve significant soft tissue component — muscle strains, tendon irritation, fascial adhesions, or post-injury scar tissue. Soft tissue therapy is an important part of Dr. Nguyen's approach to sports care. Hands-on techniques targeting the specific muscles and connective tissues involved in your injury may help reduce pain, restore mobility, and support the healing process. Individual results vary.

Recovery and Performance

Recovery from a sports injury is not just about eliminating pain — it's about restoring full function and addressing the underlying causes so the injury doesn't recur. Dr. Nguyen evaluates movement patterns, muscle imbalances, and biomechanical factors that may have contributed to your injury. A comprehensive plan typically includes adjustments and soft tissue work alongside guidance on corrective exercises, mobility work, and activity modification during recovery.

Who We Help

VAL Chiropractic serves athletes and active individuals of all backgrounds, including:

  • Recreational runners, cyclists, and triathletes
  • Team sport athletes (soccer, basketball, baseball, volleyball)
  • Strength athletes and gym-goers
  • Martial artists and combat sport athletes
  • Weekend warriors and active adults
  • Youth athletes with growing pains or sports-related injuries

Getting Back in the Game

Returning to sport after an injury requires a structured, progressive approach. Dr. Nguyen will discuss realistic timelines and milestones during your evaluation, and will work with you to develop a return-to-sport plan that balances the need for adequate recovery with your goals and schedule. Individual results vary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you treat sports injuries?

Yes. Dr. Nguyen has extensive experience treating sports-related injuries including shoulder injuries, hip pain, knee pain, ankle sprains, and back and neck pain for athletes of all levels.

Can chiropractic care help with athletic performance?

Chiropractic care is designed to help restore proper joint function, reduce muscle imbalances, and support recovery — which may help you move and perform more efficiently. Individual results vary.

How many visits will I need for a sports injury?

That depends on the nature and severity of your injury. Dr. Nguyen will assess your condition and develop an individualized care plan with realistic expectations.
