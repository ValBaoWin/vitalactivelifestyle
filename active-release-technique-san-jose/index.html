What Is Active Release Technique?

Active Release Technique (ART) is a patented soft tissue treatment method that targets muscles, tendons, ligaments, fascia, and nerves. It was developed to address the scar tissue and adhesions that can develop following injury or overuse, and which may restrict movement and cause pain.

ART combines precise manual contact with specific patient movements — the provider applies tension to the affected tissue while the patient performs active motion through a defined range. This combination is designed to break down adhesions and restore normal tissue mobility.

Conditions Commonly Addressed with Soft Tissue Therapy

  • Overuse injuries — tendinopathy, chronic muscle tightness, repetitive strain
  • Scar tissue and adhesions — post-injury or post-surgical restrictions
  • Shoulder problems — rotator cuff issues, impingement syndrome
  • Carpal tunnel syndrome — nerve entrapment from soft tissue restrictions
  • IT band and knee issues — common in runners and cyclists
  • Plantar fasciitis — fascial restrictions in the foot
