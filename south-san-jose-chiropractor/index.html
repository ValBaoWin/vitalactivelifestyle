Serving South San Jose

VAL Chiropractic is located at 3239 South White Road — accessible to residents across South San Jose and the surrounding communities. Dr. Lam Nguyen, D.C. has been serving the South Bay since opening VAL Chiropractic in 2010, and welcomes patients from South San Jose, Blossom Hill, Almaden Valley, and the broader area.

South San Jose is home to a diverse community of families, professionals, and active individuals who benefit from access to quality chiropractic care close to home. Whether you're dealing with a recent car accident injury, chronic back or neck pain, or an athletic injury, VAL Chiropractic provides the evaluation and personalized care you need.

Services Available to South San Jose Patients

From our San Jose clinic, we provide the following services to South San Jose patients: personal injury and car accident chiropractic care, whiplash treatment, sports injury chiropractic, lower back pain treatment, neck pain and headache care, sciatica evaluation and treatment, posture correction, and soft tissue therapy. Dr. Nguyen develops individualized care plans based on a thorough evaluation of your specific condition.

Personal Injury and Auto Accident Care

South San Jose residents involved in car accidents on local roads and the nearby 101, 85, and 87 freeways often seek chiropractic evaluation and care at VAL Chiropractic. Dr. Nguyen specializes in auto accident injuries and offers lien-based treatment for qualifying personal injury patients — allowing you to receive care with no upfront costs, with treatment fees paid from your settlement. We provide comprehensive medical documentation to support your personal injury claim.

About Dr. Lam Nguyen

Dr. Nguyen is a San Jose community member with over 20 years of clinical experience. He is certified in Active Release Technique (ART) and the Graston Technique, and serves as a Qualified Medical Examiner (QME) for personal injury cases. His practice philosophy focuses on identifying the root cause of pain and dysfunction — not just treating symptoms — and empowering patients with the knowledge and tools to maintain their results.
