Neck Pain: Common Causes and Our Approach

Neck pain is one of the most frequent complaints Dr. Nguyen sees at VAL Chiropractic in San Jose. It can stem from a single incident — like a car accident or a sports injury — or it can develop gradually through postural habits, repetitive stress, or ergonomic issues. The cervical spine is a complex structure, and identifying the root cause is essential to providing effective care.

Many people accept neck pain as normal — especially if they sit at a desk all day. But chronic tension and restricted movement often have addressable causes that chiropractic care may help with.

Common Causes of Neck Pain

  • Forward head posture — every inch the head shifts forward significantly increases load on the cervical spine
  • Upper cross syndrome — tight chest and neck muscles combined with weak upper back muscles
  • Whiplash — post-accident cervical injury affecting muscles, ligaments, and joints
  • Cervical spinal dysfunction — restricted segments affecting movement and potentially irritating nerves
  • Muscle tension — chronic tightness in the trapezius, levator scapulae, and suboccipital muscles
  • Disc issues — cervical disc irritation or herniation causing local or radiating symptoms

How Chiropractic Care May Help

Dr. Nguyen evaluates the cervical spine, upper thoracic spine, and surrounding musculature to identify the specific contributors to your neck pain. Care may include cervical adjustments, soft tissue work, and guidance on posture and ergonomics. Individual results vary, and Dr. Nguyen will be realistic with you about what care can address for your specific situation.

Common Questions

Neck Pain FAQs

Can chiropractic help with chronic neck pain?

Chiropractic care may address the spinal alignment and soft tissue issues contributing to neck pain. Dr. Nguyen will evaluate your specific situation and discuss a personalized approach. Individual results vary.

What is upper cross syndrome?

Upper cross syndrome is a postural pattern involving tight chest and neck muscles combined with weak upper back muscles, often contributing to forward head posture, rounded shoulders, and neck pain.

Can chiropractic help with tension headaches?

Headaches originating from neck muscle tension and cervical spine issues (cervicogenic headaches) may respond to chiropractic care. Results vary by individual.
