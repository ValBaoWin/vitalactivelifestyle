Causes of Neck Pain

Neck pain is extremely common, and the causes are varied. In many cases, neck pain develops gradually from accumulated postural stress — hours at a desk, looking at a screen, or driving. In other cases, it appears suddenly following a car accident, sports injury, or sleeping in an awkward position. Understanding the contributing factors is essential for effective treatment.

Common causes of neck pain that Dr. Nguyen evaluates and treats include cervical joint restriction and misalignment, muscle tension and trigger points, soft tissue injuries, disc irritation, nerve root compression, and postural syndromes like forward head posture and upper cross syndrome.

Forward Head Posture and Upper Cross Syndrome

Forward head posture is one of the most prevalent contributors to neck pain in modern life. Every inch the head shifts forward from its neutral position increases the effective load on the cervical spine significantly. Over time, this creates chronic strain on the posterior neck muscles and joints.

Upper cross syndrome is the broader postural pattern associated with forward head posture: tight chest muscles and neck flexors combined with weak cervical flexors and upper back muscles (rhomboids, middle and lower trapezius). This imbalance results in rounded shoulders, a forward head, and persistent neck and upper back tension that responds poorly to temporary treatments that don't address the underlying muscle pattern.

Whiplash and Accident-Related Neck Pain

Neck pain following a car accident frequently involves whiplash — a soft tissue injury caused by the rapid back-and-forth forces of a collision. Accident-related neck pain may involve muscle strains, ligament injuries, cervical joint dysfunction, and disc irritation. Early evaluation and care may help reduce the risk of these injuries becoming chronic. Dr. Nguyen also provides thorough documentation for personal injury claims.

Headaches and Neck Pain

Many headaches have a cervicogenic (neck-related) origin. Cervical joint dysfunction and muscle tension in the upper neck can refer pain into the head, creating headaches that may mimic tension headaches or even migraines. If you experience frequent headaches alongside neck pain, it is worth having the cervical spine evaluated as a contributing factor.

Chiropractic Treatment for Neck Pain

Dr. Nguyen's approach to neck pain may include cervical spinal evaluation and targeted adjustments, soft tissue work for the neck, upper back, and shoulder muscles, assessment and correction of contributing postural factors, and guidance on ergonomics, exercise, and self-care strategies.

Soft Tissue Work and Posture Correction

Soft tissue techniques targeting the muscles and fascia of the neck, upper back, and shoulders can play an important role in neck pain relief and recovery. Combined with postural assessment and corrective strategies, this approach addresses both the immediate pain and the contributing factors. Individual results vary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can chiropractic help with chronic neck pain?

Chiropractic care may address the spinal alignment and soft tissue issues contributing to neck pain. Dr. Nguyen will evaluate your specific situation and discuss a personalized approach.

What is upper cross syndrome?

Upper cross syndrome is a postural pattern involving tight chest and neck muscles combined with weak upper back muscles, often contributing to forward head posture, rounded shoulders, and neck pain.

Can chiropractic care help with headaches caused by neck tension?
