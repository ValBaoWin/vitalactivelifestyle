What Is Whiplash?

Whiplash is a neck injury caused by a rapid, forceful back-and-forth motion of the head — most commonly during a rear-end car accident. The sudden acceleration-deceleration forces can strain or damage the muscles, tendons, ligaments, and cervical spinal joints in the neck.

At VAL Chiropractic in San Jose, Dr. Lam Nguyen, D.C. evaluates and treats whiplash injuries resulting from car accidents and other trauma.

Whiplash can affect the neck, upper back, shoulders, and even cause headaches — getting evaluated promptly is important even if you feel okay immediately after the accident.

Common Whiplash Symptoms

  • Neck pain and stiffness — often the primary complaint, sometimes beginning hours after the accident
  • Reduced range of motion — difficulty turning or tilting the head
  • Headaches — typically starting at the base of the skull and radiating forward
  • Shoulder and upper back pain — from referred muscle tension and ligament strain
  • Arm tingling or numbness — may indicate nerve involvement from cervical irritation
  • Fatigue and difficulty concentrating — commonly reported in the days following a collision

How Whiplash Is Evaluated and Treated

Dr. Nguyen performs a thorough evaluation including your accident history, physical examination, and orthopedic testing. He then develops a personalized care plan that may include spinal adjustments to the cervical spine, soft tissue therapy, and rehabilitation guidance. The goal is to support recovery and help prevent short-term injuries from becoming chronic problems. Individual results vary.

Whiplash Documentation for Personal Injury Claims

Thorough documentation of your whiplash injury is important for your personal injury claim. Dr. Nguyen provides detailed injury reports, treatment records, and progress notes that your attorney may use to support your case. Lien-based care may be available for qualifying patients.

Whiplash FAQs

Can chiropractic care help with whiplash?

Chiropractic evaluation and treatment may help address the spinal misalignment and soft tissue damage associated with whiplash. Individual results vary.

How soon after an accident should I get checked for whiplash?

As soon as possible. Whiplash symptoms often appear hours or days after a collision. Early evaluation may reduce the risk of long-term problems.

Dr. Nguyen performs a thorough evaluation, then develops a personalized plan that may include spinal adjustments, soft tissue therapy, and guided rehabilitation exercises.
