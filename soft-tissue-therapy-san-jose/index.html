What Is Soft Tissue Therapy?

Soft tissue therapy encompasses a range of hands-on techniques applied to the muscles, tendons, ligaments, and fascia of the body. These techniques are used to address restrictions, adhesions, scar tissue, and overuse-related changes that can contribute to pain, limited mobility, and poor movement quality.

At VAL Chiropractic in San Jose, soft tissue therapy is integrated with chiropractic adjustments as part of a comprehensive care approach. Dr. Lam Nguyen, D.C. uses soft tissue work to complement spinal care — addressing both the joint and the surrounding muscle and fascia together.

Soft tissue therapy at VAL Chiropractic is targeted and specific — based on what Dr. Nguyen identifies in your evaluation — rather than a generalized relaxation approach.

Who Benefits from Soft Tissue Therapy?

  • Athletes and active adults — addressing muscle tightness, overuse issues, and aiding sports recovery
  • Personal injury patients — treating soft tissue damage from car accidents or trauma
  • Office workers and desk-based professionals — addressing chronic muscle tension from prolonged sitting and screen work
  • People with repetitive strain issues — tendinopathy, forearm/wrist tightness, shoulder impingement patterns
  • Post-injury rehabilitation — supporting recovery from sprains, strains, and surgical procedures

Soft Tissue Techniques at VAL Chiropractic

Dr. Nguyen may incorporate various forms of soft tissue work depending on your specific needs. These may include myofascial release, trigger point therapy, instrument-assisted soft tissue mobilization, and manual stretching techniques. The specific approach is tailored to what your evaluation reveals about your condition. Please call us to discuss what techniques may be most appropriate for your situation.

Common Questions

Soft Tissue Therapy FAQs

What is soft tissue therapy?

Soft tissue therapy refers to hands-on techniques applied to muscles, tendons, ligaments, and fascia to address restrictions, tightness, and scar tissue. It may support mobility, reduce discomfort, and support recovery.

Who benefits from soft tissue therapy?

Athletes, people recovering from injuries, individuals with overuse or repetitive stress issues, and those with post-accident soft tissue damage may benefit from soft tissue work.

Is soft tissue therapy the same as massage?

While there is overlap, soft tissue therapy in a chiropractic setting is typically more targeted — addressing specific dysfunctions identified in your evaluation — and is integrated with your overall care plan.
